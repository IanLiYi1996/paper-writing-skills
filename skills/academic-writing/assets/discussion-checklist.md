# Discussion Section Checklist

Use this checklist while drafting and revising your Discussion section. For detailed guidance, see [discussion-guide.md](../references/discussion-guide.md).

---

## Pre-Writing

- [ ] Reviewed all Results section content thoroughly
- [ ] Listed 3-5 main findings to discuss in order of importance
- [ ] Identified 5-7 relevant studies for comparison
- [ ] Noted any unexpected or counter-intuitive findings
- [ ] Brainstormed 2-3 theoretical or practical implications
- [ ] Listed 3-5 genuine limitations (methodological boundaries, not minor issues)

---

## Step 1: Summarize Current Research

- [ ] Opened with concise summary of main findings (2-4 sentences)
- [ ] Avoided repeating Results section verbatim or listing specific numbers
- [ ] Connected summary to original research questions or hypotheses
- [ ] Used appropriate certainty language (demonstrates, suggests, indicates -- not "proves")
- [ ] Did not introduce any new data not mentioned in Results
- [ ] Provided high-level interpretation, not just restatement

---

## Step 2: Relate Results to Existing Literature

- [ ] Compared findings with at least 3-5 highly relevant studies
- [ ] Acknowledged findings that align with previous research
- [ ] Addressed findings that diverge from or contradict prior work
- [ ] Provided plausible explanations for discrepancies
- [ ] Showed how work builds upon, extends, or complements existing research
- [ ] Highlighted what makes contribution unique
- [ ] Integrated citations throughout (not just at beginning)
- [ ] Been fair to prior work
- [ ] Used varied transitional phrases

---

## Step 3: Address Unexpected Findings

- [ ] Identified genuinely unexpected results
- [ ] Provided evidence-based explanations for surprises
- [ ] Discussed potential methodological factors
- [ ] Framed unexpected findings constructively (as insights, not failures)
- [ ] Avoided defensive or dismissive language
- [ ] Connected unexpected findings to broader implications

---

## Step 4: Broader Implications

- [ ] Linked findings to relevant theoretical frameworks
- [ ] Explained how findings advance understanding of the phenomenon
- [ ] Discussed at least 1-2 practical applications
- [ ] Articulated what findings mean for the field beyond this study
- [ ] Balanced specificity (grounded in results) with generality (broader significance)
- [ ] Addressed both theoretical and practical contributions
- [ ] Avoided overgeneralizing beyond what data support
- [ ] Connected to ongoing debates or open questions

---

## Step 5: Limitations

- [ ] Listed 3-5 genuine limitations (methodological constraints, not minor issues)
- [ ] Framed each constructively (boundary of generalization, not fatal flaw)
- [ ] Explained how each might affect interpretation
- [ ] Connected limitations to specific future research directions
- [ ] Maintained confidence in core contributions
- [ ] Avoided excessive self-criticism or self-defeating language
- [ ] Distinguished limitations (unavoidable boundaries) from weaknesses (correctable flaws)
- [ ] Did not introduce limitations that completely undermine contribution
- [ ] Provided context for why certain limitations exist

---

## Overall Quality

- [ ] Follows logical flow: summary -> literature -> implications -> limitations
- [ ] Each paragraph has clear topic sentence and focused content
- [ ] Transitional phrases connect paragraphs smoothly
- [ ] No contradictions with other sections (especially Introduction and Results)
- [ ] Appropriate hedging (suggests, indicates) rather than absolute claims
- [ ] Clear and accessible, avoiding unnecessary jargon
- [ ] Appropriate length (typically 20-30% of paper body)
- [ ] All claims supported by data or cited from literature
- [ ] Provides interpretation and insight, not just summary
- [ ] Future work suggestions are specific and actionable
- [ ] Addresses "so what?" -- why findings matter
- [ ] Concludes with strong takeaway that sets up Conclusion

---

## Integration with Other Sections

- [ ] Addresses all research questions from Introduction
- [ ] Findings relate to gaps identified in Introduction
- [ ] Contributions claimed in Introduction are substantiated
- [ ] All major Results are interpreted (nothing presented but ignored)
- [ ] Specific Results (tables, figures) referenced when interpreting
- [ ] Consistent terminology across Abstract, Introduction, Results, Discussion

---

## Language and Style

- [ ] Active voice where appropriate ("We found..." not "It was found...")
- [ ] Varied sentence structure
- [ ] Eliminated redundant phrases
- [ ] Avoided absolute statements ("always," "never," "proves")
- [ ] Consistent terminology
- [ ] Minimized ambiguous pronouns (clarified referents)
- [ ] Appropriate disciplinary tone and conventions
- [ ] All technical terms defined or previously introduced

---

## Final Checks

- [ ] Read entire Discussion aloud to check flow
- [ ] Verified all citations are correct and properly formatted
- [ ] Confirmed Discussion does not repeat Results unnecessarily
- [ ] No new data or results introduced in Discussion
- [ ] Had colleague review for clarity and completeness
- [ ] Limitations do not overshadow contributions
- [ ] All future work suggestions are feasible and specific

---

## Quick Self-Assessment

1. **Clarity**: Can a colleague understand main arguments without the full paper?
2. **Depth**: Have I gone beyond summarizing to truly interpreting?
3. **Context**: Have I positioned my work within existing literature?
4. **Honesty**: Have I acknowledged limitations without undermining the contribution?
5. **Impact**: Is it clear why findings matter and what comes next?
6. **Coherence**: Does the Discussion tell a logical story from findings to implications?
